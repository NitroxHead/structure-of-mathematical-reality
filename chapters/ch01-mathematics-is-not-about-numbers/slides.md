# Chapter 1: Mathematics Is Not About Numbers
## Phase I: What Mathematics Is
### Slides

---

## What Mathematics Is Not

- Not numbers. Not computation. Not "quantitative reasoning."
- Those are *outputs* of mathematics, not what it *is*.
- The number line you were handed as a child is a picture, not a definition.
- Your suspicion that it is a prejudice, not a fact, was correct.

## The One-Sentence Definition

> Mathematics is the study of structure, stripped of material content.

- Study the *pattern*, throw away the things that carry it.
- Rotations of a square, a shuffled deck, a crystal's symmetries: one structure.
- Content changes; structure does not.

## One Equation, Three Systems

- Radioactive isotope, dying cell culture, discharging capacitor.
- All obey: `dN/dt = -k N` (rate of change proportional to amount).
- Solution: exponential decay, same curve, different labels.
- Not coincidence, not mysticism: they *instantiate the same structure*.

## Why Math Grips The World

- Domains are not secretly numerical.
- They contain *structures*, and structures recur across unrelated systems.
- A structure extracted in one place transfers to another.
- Material does not travel; structure does. (Chapter 6 presses this harder.)

## The Organizing Principle

> Every field of mathematics is defined by what kind of structure it preserves.

- Not by its objects. By what it holds fixed while all else varies.
- The list of fields is the map; this sentence is the compass.
- Regenerate the map from the compass, don't memorize it.

## The Fields, By What They Fix

| Field | Structure preserved |
|---|---|
| Algebra | operations (combine, follow rules) |
| Analysis | limits (approach, converge, infinity) |
| Geometry | rigidity (distance, angle) |
| Topology | continuity (deform, no tear or glue) |
| Logic | reasoning itself (proof, computation) |
| Probability | what holds under uncertainty |

## Geometry vs Topology

- Same objects (shapes); different meaning of "the same shape."
- Geometry: same if distances and angles match.
- Topology: same if connectivity matches (coffee cup = doughnut).
- Choosing which to preserve chooses which tools apply.

## Objects, Questions, Maps

- Each field has a type of object and a characteristic question.
- Deepest: its type of *map*, a function respecting the fixed structure.
- Rigid motions, continuous deformations, sum-preserving maps.
- The maps carry more than the objects. (Chapter 16 is built on this.)

## The Key Shift

> Mathematics studies relationships between things, not the things.

- Relationships have their own structure: more stable, more transportable.
- The number 3 is a *position*, not a quantity of stuff.
- Objects defined by structural role, not intrinsic content.

## Modeling Is Structure Extraction

- Your model does not capture the biology; it captures a *structural relationship*.
- Works when that structure is really present; fails when it isn't.
- "Linear regression fits" proves a linear structure in *your sampled slice*.
- It does NOT prove the system is linear. Map is not territory.

## When An Operation Breaks

- Averaging assumes a real magnitude scale AND concentration around a typical value.
- Fails on labels (mean of cell-type codes is meaningless).
- Fails with no representative value (bimodal cells, heavy tails, no finite mean).
- A broken operation is a probe: it exposes the missing structure.

## Is Math A Language? Discovered Or Invented?

- Language part is right: notation, grammar, compression.
- Breaks here: symbols are convention; *consequences* are forced.
- Invented: the choice of structure and notation.
- Discovered: everything that structure then forces (reals are uncountable).

## Where You Now Stand

- Math = the study of structure; each field fixes one kind; structure transfers.
- You can now separate a chosen structure from its forced consequences.
