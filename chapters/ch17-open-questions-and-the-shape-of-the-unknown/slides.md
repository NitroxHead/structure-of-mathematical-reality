# Chapter 17: Open Questions and the Shape of the Unknown
## Phase V: The Connective Tissue
### Slides

---

## Three Territories, Kept Apart

- What you now know.
- What you do not know but could learn from an existing book.
- What nobody knows, because the question is genuinely open.
- Blurring these makes the unknown feel larger than it is.

> Giving the unknown a shape is the whole work of this chapter.

## The Framework, In Thirteen Lines (1)

- Math is the study of structure (Ch 1).
- Objects are defined by role, not substance (Ch 2, 7, 8).
- Fields are organized by structure preserved; bridges are functors (Ch 3, 16).
- Proof is mechanical, and every formal system has limits (Ch 4, Godel).
- Symmetry = power; its absence = provable hardness (Ch 5).
- Effectiveness is partly explained, partly still mysterious (Ch 6, 11).

## The Framework, In Thirteen Lines (2)

- Linearity is a symmetry of the canvas; effective because systems are smooth (Ch 5, 8, 10).
- Change has structure: flows, fixed points, bifurcations, chaos (Ch 10).
- Probability: reasoning under uncertainty, with a contested foundation (Ch 11).
- Measurement is the first homomorphism (Ch 13).
- Models are structure-preserving maps; they fail three ways (Ch 14).
- Compression and understanding are one activity (Ch 12, 15).
- Category theory states all of it precisely (Ch 16).

## They Interlock

- Symmetry (5) explains why linearity (7) is powerful.
- A linear model's failure (11) is a bifurcation (10).
- Universality (6, 9) is why coarse models (11) and measurement (10) work.
- Compression (12) is what a model (11) does at its best.
- The map is connected. That was the point.

## Vocabulary: One Experiment, Many Chapters

- A dose-response curve: flat, then a steep switch, then saturation.
- "Can I take the mean?" is a scale question (Ch 13).
- "Why modelable despite the mess?" coarse-graining, universality (Ch 11).
- "Why does it switch?" a bifurcation in a nonlinear system (Ch 10).
- "Fit fails at the extremes" boundary failure; linearity is local (Ch 14, 8).

## Vocabulary: Naming The Experience

- "This method feels powerful": it exploits a symmetry (Ch 5).
- "Brute force, can't speed up": maybe no symmetry to exploit (Ch 5).
- "Short equation fits": candidate compression, maybe not the mechanism (Ch 12, 15).
- "Works but I can't say why": faithful map, features not yet identified (Ch 14).
- "Deep question or badly posed?": the skill this chapter trains.

## Open Question 1: Compressibility

- Why are the fundamental laws expressible as short equations?
- Universality is a *genuine partial* answer, not a resolution.
- It explains recurrence given a well-behaved coarse-graining.
- It does NOT explain why the fundamental laws are compressible at all.

> The residue has no consensus answer. It stays open.

## Open Questions 2 and 3

- **Right foundation?** ZFC is the default; type theory, category theory, homotopy type theory are live alternatives. Active, unsettled.
- **Discovered or invented?** The shallow version dissolves: notation is invented, consequences are discovered (Ch 1).
- The deep version stays open: are the structures themselves out there, or artifacts of how we carve reality?

## Open Questions 4, 5, 6

- **Complexity of finding structure?** Your own question. Solomonoff is uncomputable; ties to P vs. NP (Ch 5, 15). Still a frontier.
- **Why is correct math beautiful?** Bias, selection effect, or something deeper? No one knows.
- **What is probability?** Kolmogorov's calculus is settled; frequency vs. belief vs. measure is not (Ch 11).

## Open Is Not One Thing

- Some ask why world and mind are arranged as they are (1, 5).
- Some ask which working system is best (2).
- Some may be malformed and need reframing (3).
- Some are hard in a way complexity might make precise (4).
- One has a settled formalism and a contested meaning (6).

## Gaps: Doors, Not Walls

- Differential geometry, Lie theory, measure theory, functional analysis.
- Ergodic theory, computational complexity, algebraic topology.
- Graph theory and networks: the largest unmapped daily-relevant region (PCM III.34).
- Duality: the shadow-structure lens you used unnamed (PCM III.19).
- Model theory: where "what a model is" gets its deepest treatment.
- You now enter each with a specific question, not by wandering in.

## Your Five Questions, Answered

- **What is math?** The study of structure, plus its structure-preserving maps.
- **Why does it work?** A model shares the system's relevant structure; structure transfers, material does not.
- **What are numbers?** Positions in a structure; ℕ → ℤ → ℚ → ℝ → ℂ is forced closure.
- **How do fields relate?** By functors: bridges that carry objects, maps, and reasoning.
- **When a model works?** Its structure faithfully mirrors enough of the system's.

## Where You Now Stand

- You hold orientation, not mastery. That was always the goal.
- Hit a wall: locate it, name its kind, know which way to move.
- Elegant solution or provable limit? Deep question or badly posed? A settled door or an open edge?
- The formless unknown now has a shape: a coastline of open questions, a settled interior, labeled doors outward.
> You did not set out to become a mathematician. You set out to build a map you could steer by, and you now hold one. Where you go next is your choice.
